Do Repeat Yourself

February 10, 2022 · One min read

DRY (don’t repeat yourself) is common among the first software development principles we learn. It’s the principle that leads to cleaner, more compact and readable code bases. Or, is it the principle that leads computer science students and misguided programmers into redundant (over)abstractions, and code which is hard and complex to refactor?

Every piece of knowledge must have a single, unambiguous, authoritative representation within a system

Another approach to abstractions is the AHA principle. AHA stands for "avoid hasty abstractions", described by Kent C. Dodds as optimizing for change first, and avoiding premature optimization.[8] and was influenced by Sandi Metz's "prefer duplication over the wrong abstraction".
